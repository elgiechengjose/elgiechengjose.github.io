Curving a Bowling Ball

One thing I have been asked is how to curve a bowling ball. It is pretty simple if you understand the mechanics on how to do it. It will take a lot of time and practice to curve a bowling ball consistently. The steps below will, also, help prevent you from injuring yourself:

  1. Ensure your wrist is straight

  2. Keep hand straight when you swing your arm back

  3. As your arm comes forward, begin to rotate your wrist, hand and fingers toward the opposite side of your body (right-handers turn to left; left-handers turn to the right)

  4. Release the ball in front or near your ankle and finish in the hand shake position after ball is out of your hand

As long as you follow these four easy steps, you will be able to curve a bowling ball without any injuries. Below is a video from Professional Bowlers Brad Miller and Kyle Sherman on their take on how to curve a bowling ball.


Picking the Right Bowling Ball

A bowling ball is a must need to knock down all the pins. However, deciding on what bowling ball you need is a little bit more trickier. So, how do we decide what the right bowling ball is for us?

10% Rule

This rule states that you should choose a bowling based on 10% of your body weight. If you are 150 lbs, you should choose a 15 lb bowling ball (max weight of bowling ball is 16 lbs).

**However if you have had an injury to prevent you from using that weight or it feels too heavy, lower the weight to prvent any injury or further injury.**

Finger Holes Fit Just Right

Make sure when you are choosing a ball the finger holes are not too small and not too big. The fitting can change the difference to the game.

Do Not Overstretch Your Hand

Your hand should not feel like it is overstretched. You should feel a light touch between your palm and the ball. It will help you control the ball without having you to grip the ball too tightly.

Above are ways of selecting a bowling ball when you are going to your nearby bowling alley.
